## What the data is designed to reveal

1. **The precision/recall gap is a gold-size-vs-k artifact, not a "mediocre retriever."**
   At fixed k=10, precision@10 ≤ |gold|/10 and recall@10 ≤ 10/|gold|. Small-gold categories
   (Cafe, Cheap eats) therefore show *high recall, low precision*; the large-gold category
   (Fine dining) shows the opposite. Strong candidates explain the ~0.48 / ~0.68 split this
   way rather than blaming retriever quality.

2. **Macro vs micro averaging diverge.** Macro recall (≈0.68) treats every query equally;
   micro recall (≈0.61) pools all hits/gold and is dragged down because Fine dining's large
   gold sets dominate the denominator. A candidate who reports one number as *the* number and
   never notices the other is showing a blind spot.

3. **Slicing beats aggregates.** Neither planted problem below is visible in a single headline
   number — both require grouping by `category` (and, for Vegan, comparing across `runs`).

## Planted patterns

Two **orthogonal** failure modes, one per metric family:

1. **Japanese → rank-placement trap.** Relevant restaurants *are* retrieved and *are*
   perfectly stable across the 3 runs (Jaccard 1.00), but they consistently land at
   **ranks 6–10**. So set metrics look normal (precision 0.50, recall 0.71) while
   **rank-aware metrics collapse (nDCG 0.54, MRR 0.17)**. This is the case that makes
   nDCG/MRR earn their place — without it they just track precision. *Requires a rank-aware
   metric + slicing by category to find.*

2. **Vegan → determinism + quality trap.** The 3 runs return **substantially different
   result sets** (mean pairwise Jaccard **0.24** vs ~0.85–1.00 everywhere else) **and**
   the quality is worse (precision 0.29, recall 0.41). *Requires a cross-run agreement
   metric (e.g. mean pairwise Jaccard) plus slicing by category to find.* The two signals
   are coupled by design (unstable *and* worse), which is realistic and lets the candidate
   hypothesize a cause.

Together they demonstrate that **determinism ≠ quality**: Japanese is stable but badly
ranked; Vegan is unstable and poor. All other categories are stable (high Jaccard) with
healthy metrics, serving as the reference baseline.

## Discussion hooks / what to credit

- **Only 3 runs per query.** Enough for three pairwise comparisons, but coarse — credit a
  candidate who flags that 3 runs is thin evidence to *firmly conclude* a category is
  nondeterministic.
- **Set-level vs metric-level instability differ.** Jaccard measures whether the *sets*
  change; the std-dev of recall across runs measures whether the *score* changes. They do
  not always coincide (swapping one relevant item for another equally-relevant one changes
  the set but not precision). Credit a candidate who distinguishes them.
- **sklearn fit.** The rank/set metrics here (recall@k, MRR, nDCG@k over ranked ID lists,
  Jaccard across runs) don't map cleanly onto sklearn's matrix-oriented helpers. A candidate
  who notices this and implements them directly (or pushes back on "must use sklearn") is
  showing good judgment, not cutting corners.
